Apples

Apples are a type of fruit that grow on trees of the species Malus domestica. They come in a variety of colors, including red, green, and yellow, and are a popular food due to their sweet taste and crunchy texture.

Nutrition

Apples are a good source of fiber, vitamins, and minerals. They are low in calories and high in antioxidants, which can help to protect the body from damage caused by free radicals. Some studies have also linked regular consumption of apples to a reduced risk of certain health problems, such as heart disease and type 2 diabetes.

Uses

Apples can be eaten raw or cooked, and are used in a variety of recipes, such as pies, cakes, and sauces. They are also a popular snack food and can be dried or juiced. In addition to their culinary uses, apples have a long history of being used in traditional medicine to treat a range of ailments.
